Hidden away from the prying eyes of the mundane world, Evertown was a most curious place indeed. Nestled between rolling hills and ancient forests, it was home to exactly thirteen inhabitants—no more, no less. The townsfolk had an uncanny fondness for forming clubs, gatherings where tales were spun and secrets whispered under the glow of candlelight. Yet, just as much as they loved their clubs, they adored crafting intricate rules to govern them. One such rule was quite straightforward: every new club must have at least one member and no two clubs will have the exact same set of members. The second rule, however, was delightfully peculiar. It decreed that any new club must share at least one member with every existing club. In Evertown, isolation was unheard of; connections wove through the community like threads in a tapestry. All new clubs were meticulously recorded by Mr. Every, the diligent keeper of records at the town hall. A tall man with twinkling eyes behind half-moon spectacles, he harbored a deep love for the art of discrete mathematics, despite never having attended a day at any wizarding academy—a consequence of Evertown's eccentric traditions of course. For the past week, Mr. Every had been consumed by a perplexing riddle. He pondered endlessly: what was the maximum number of clubs that could flourish in Evertown while honoring the town's whimsical rules? The question danced in his mind like golden snitches on a Quidditch field. Could someone perhaps aid Mr. Every in unraveling this enigma by determining the precise number, k, and providing irrefutable proof that k clubs can indeed exist, but not a single one more?
